# Amazon Company Profile — Business Intelligence Knowledge Base

## Company Overview
Amazon.com, Inc. (NASDAQ: AMZN) is the world's largest e-commerce marketplace and cloud computing company, founded by Jeff Bezos in 1994 as an online bookstore. Headquartered in Seattle, Washington, Amazon has grown into a diversified technology conglomerate operating in e-commerce, artificial intelligence, cloud infrastructure, and digital streaming.

## Business Segments

### North America E-commerce
Amazon North America is the company's largest revenue segment, generating over $400 billion annually. The platform hosts over 9.7 million third-party sellers who collectively account for roughly 60% of unit sales. Amazon's first-party retail competes directly with third-party sellers using the same platform.

### Third-Party Marketplace (Seller Services)
Amazon Marketplace allows third-party sellers to reach Amazon's 300+ million active customers. Key services include:
- Fulfillment by Amazon (FBA): Sellers store inventory in Amazon warehouses; Amazon handles picking, packing, and delivery. Sellers pay fulfillment fees but gain Prime eligibility.
- Amazon Selling Partner API: Enables sellers to programmatically manage inventory, orders, and reports.
- Brand Registry: Protects intellectual property for registered brands and enables enhanced brand content.

### Amazon Prime
Amazon Prime (~$139/year in the US) is the company's loyalty engine with over 200 million global subscribers. Benefits include:
- Free two-day shipping (upgraded to one-day for most members)
- Prime Video streaming
- Prime Music
- Prime Gaming
- Prime Reading
- Whole Foods market access
- Prime Day exclusive deals

### Amazon Web Services (AWS)
AWS is the global market leader in cloud infrastructure services, generating over $90 billion in annual revenue. AWS serves millions of active customers including Netflix, government agencies, and startups. Key services include:
- Compute (EC2), Storage (S3), Databases (RDS, DynamoDB)
- AI/ML services (SageMaker, Bedrock with Llama and Claude models)
- Amazon Alexa voice platform

### Amazon Advertising
Amazon Ads has grown into a $50+ billion annual business, making it the third-largest digital advertising platform behind Google and Meta. Key ad products include:
- Sponsored Products: Auction-based product ads in search results
- Sponsored Brands: Showcase brand and product selection
- Amazon DSP: Programmatic advertising for reaching audiences on and off Amazon
- Video ads on Prime Video

### Amazon Logistics (Last-Mile Delivery)
Amazon has built the world's largest last-mile delivery network, delivering over 5.9 billion packages in 2023. The network includes:
- Amazon Delivery Service Partners (DSPs): Entrepreneurs who run their own delivery businesses using Amazon-branded vehicles
- Amazon Flex: Gig workers delivering packages using personal vehicles
- Amazon Logistics (AMZL): Amazon's own delivery service
- Amazon Air: Cargo airline operating in major US hubs
- Project Kuiper: Satellite-based broadband network for rural delivery coordination

## Strengths
- Dominant e-commerce market share: 38% of US e-commerce sales
- Prime ecosystem creates powerful customer lock-in and purchase frequency
- AWS provides capital for logistics investment while generating premium margins
- World's most advanced fulfillment infrastructure with predictive inventory positioning
- Superior data on consumer purchasing behavior at scale
- Alexa voice assistant ecosystem with 500+ million devices sold

## Weaknesses
- Third-party seller trust issues: counterfeits, quality inconsistency, and fee increases
- Heavy regulatory scrutiny in US, EU, and other markets
- FBA costs increasing significantly, pushing sellers to alternatives
- Workplace conditions have faced criticism from employees and regulators
- Heavy reliance on advertising revenue from third-party sellers

## Opportunities
- Healthcare expansion: Amazon Pharmacy, One Medical acquisition ($3.9B)
- International growth in India, Southeast Asia, and Middle East
- B2B marketplace (Amazon Business) growing rapidly
- AI/foundation model leadership through Amazon Bedrock
- Robotics automation in fulfillment centers (Acoustic Intelligence)

## Threats
- Walmart's omnichannel strategy leveraging physical store fulfillment
- Temu and Shein price competition from Chinese ultra-fast-fashion brands
- TikTok Shop social commerce capturing younger demographics
- EU Digital Markets Act (DMA) forcing interoperability requirements
- US antitrust legislation targeting marketplace practices
- Economic downturn reducing consumer discretionary spending
