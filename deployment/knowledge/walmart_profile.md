# Walmart Inc. Company Profile — Business Intelligence Knowledge Base

## Company Overview
Walmart Inc. (NYSE: WMT) is the world's largest company by revenue, generating over $665 billion in annual revenue (FY 2024). Founded by Sam Walton in 1962 in Rogers, Arkansas, Walmart has grown from a single discount store into a global omnichannel retailer operating over 10,500 stores across 19 countries. Headquartered in Bentonville, Arkansas, Walmart is the largest private employer in the world with approximately 2.1 million employees.

## Business Segments

### US Walmart
The US segment is Walmart's largest, accounting for approximately 70% of total revenue:
- 4,600+ stores across all 50 US states
- 3,400+ pickup points and 3,500+ grocery pickup towers
- 1,900+ stores offering same-day delivery
- 47 Sam's Club warehouse stores

### Walmart International
International operations span 19 countries with significant positions in:
- Mexico (Walmart de Mexico — Walmex, largest single-country operation)
- Canada (310+ stores)
- Central America (Costa Rica, Guatemala, Honduras, El Salvador, Nicaragua)
- China (through majority-owned JD.com stake)
- India (through Flipkart — $21B acquisition in 2018)
- Africa (Massmart acquisition)

### Sam's Club
Sam's Club is Walmart's warehouse club format:
- 680+ clubs in the US
- Membership-based (Club and Plus tiers)
- Competing with Costco and BJ's Wholesale
- 75+ million members globally

## E-commerce Strategy

### Walmart E-commerce Growth
Walmart's US e-commerce business is one of the fastest-growing segments:
- FY2024 e-commerce revenue growth: 24% YoY
- Global e-commerce GMV: $100+ billion
- Pickup: 90% of US population lives within 10 miles of a Walmart store
- Delivery: Walmart GoLocal, DoorDash, and Uber Eats partnerships

### Walmart+
Walmart's answer to Amazon Prime launched in September 2020:
- $98/year subscription ($12.95/month)
- Competing directly with Amazon Prime
- Key benefits: Unlimited free grocery delivery, fuel discounts at 2,000+ stations, Paramount+ streaming
- Member count estimated at 32+ million (vs Amazon's 200M+ Prime)
- Focus on grocery and household essentials where Walmart has the price advantage

### Store-as-Warehouse Model
Walmart's unique omnichannel competitive advantage:
- 4,600+ US stores serve as local fulfillment centers
- Enables same-day pickup and delivery without dedicated dark stores
- Reduces last-mile delivery cost by 30-50% vs pure-play e-commerce
- Store associates pick online orders using Walmart's proprietary mobile app
- Micro-fulfillment centers adjacent to high-volume stores

### Marketplace Expansion
Walmart Marketplace is growing rapidly:
- 100,000+ third-party sellers (vs Amazon's 9.7 million — significant room to grow)
- Selective acceptance to maintain quality standards
- No FBA equivalent (sellers fulfill directly to Walmart's standards)
- Growing marketplace GMV faster than first-party e-commerce

## Technology and AI

### Luminate Platform
Walmart's proprietary retail data and AI platform:
- Unifies online and in-store data for 265+ million annual customers
- Demand forecasting for inventory optimization
- Eden: AI-driven markdown optimization and pricing
- Real-time shelf monitoring using computer vision

### Walmart Global Tech
- 25,000+ engineers globally
- Investment in automation, robotics, and supply chain AI
- Building proprietary LLM for internal and customer-facing applications

### Autonomous Vehicles
- Partnership with Cruise (GM) for autonomous delivery pilots
- Udaily (Gatik) for short-haul autonomous delivery between stores

## Financial Profile

### Key Metrics (FY2024)
- Total Revenue: $665 billion
- US E-commerce growth: 24% YoY
- Grocery market share: ~27% of US grocery spending
- Operating income: $27 billion
- Free cash flow: $15+ billion annually

### Competitive Positioning
- Low Price Leader: Everyday Low Price (EDLP) strategy
- Private label strength: Great Value, Sam's Club Member's Mark
- Grocery dominance: Largest US grocery retailer by far
- Ad business: Walmart Connect growing rapidly as CTV advertising platform

## Strengths
- 4,600+ US stores = unique last-mile delivery cost advantage
- Dominant grocery position (27% US grocery share) driving high purchase frequency
- Strong private label brands with superior margins
- Supply chain excellence with industry-leading logistics
- Growing advertising revenue (Walmart Connect) with retail media network
- International footprint in high-growth markets (India via Flipkart, Mexico via Walmex)

## Weaknesses
- E-commerce technology and UX still lags Amazon
- Limited marketplace scale relative to competitors
- Lower brand perception for non-grocery products
- International operations inconsistent (sold UK Asda, exited Japan)
- Limited premium brand assortment vs Amazon

## Opportunities
- Converting stores into micro-fulfillment centers for faster delivery
- Flipkart's growth in India's $200B+ e-commerce market
- Walmart Connect advertising revenue scaling to compete with Amazon Ads
- Healthcare expansion: Walmart Health clinics in select markets
- Generative AI for customer service, search, and product discovery

## Threats
- Amazon Fresh and Whole Foods targeting Walmart's grocery crown
- Dollar stores (Dollar General, Dollar Tree) capturing rural price-sensitive customers
- Chinese fast-fashion platforms (Temu, Shein) undercutting on price
- Instacart and DoorDash competition for grocery delivery
- Economic downturn reducing consumer spending in Walmart's core customer base
